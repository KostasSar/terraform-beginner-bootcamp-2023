Espresso Recipe

1. Get some freshly roasted beans

Start with high-quality, freshly roasted coffee beans.

2. Weigh on a scale

Use a scale to measure the precise amount of coffee grounds you need.

3. RDT

The Ross Droplet Technique (RDT) involves adding a few drops of water to coffee beans before grinding to prevent static electricity, clumping, and promote a more even distribution of grounds, leading to a more consistent and efficient extraction when brewing espresso.

4. Load and grind

Load your beans in the grinder and start grinding.

5. Place grounds in basket and distribute

Place the grounds in the espresso basket and distribute using a WDT tool. Even distribution will increase extraction and produce a smooth cup of coffee.

6. Tamp

Using an tamper, press down on your grounds to make an even coffee bed in your bsaket.

7. Puckscreen

Use a puckscreen to make water distribution more even on your coffee cake and keep your coffee machine clean.

8. Lock portafilter in

Lock the portafilter on the espresso machine and wait for it to reach the desired temperature.

9. Brew

Let your coffee brew. We are aiming for a brew time of 25-30 seconds with a 1:2 ratio. 17 grams of coffee went into the basket and we are expecting 34 grams in our glass.

10. Enjoy

Enjoy a beautiful shot of espresso.
